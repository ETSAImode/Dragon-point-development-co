Project Two
The Dragon Point Restoration

Ironworks sets a high bar. So what do you do for an encore? You choose a project that doesn't just develop land—it restores a legend. Dragon Point is the most recognized landmark in Brevard County history. A fire-breathing dragon sculpture that stood for three decades at the southern tip of Merritt Island, where the Indian and Banana Rivers meet, visible to every boater on the lagoon.

She fell in 2002. Developers have tried and failed to bring her back three times. We intend to succeed—because this isn't just a project. This is a statement. When the locals rally behind your name, you can build anything you want next.

Annie — The Legend of Dragon Point
The Peninsula — Indian River Lagoon
0.86 Acres — 1,398 ft Waterfront
1971
Artist Lewis VanDercar sculpts "Annie"—a 35-foot, 65-foot-long, 20-ton concrete and steel dragon. She becomes an instant Brevard icon.
1974
Annie breathes fire for the first time. Red eyes glow at night. Propane flamethrower lights up the lagoon at Fourth of July celebrations.
1981
Warren McFadden expands the legend: extended neck, tail, cave people, and four hatchlings. 27,000 visitors come through.
2002
Annie collapses into the lagoon during a storm, worsened by vandalism. Three subsequent attempts to rebuild her all fail.
Now
Dragon Point Development Co. acquires the site. 0.86 acres. 1,398 feet of waterfront. 180-degree views. One mission: bring the dragon home.
